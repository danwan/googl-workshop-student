# 🔵 Lab 5.2 — The Idea Machine

> **Your mission:** Sit down with a pre-built brainstorming agent that never runs dry, and walk away with **10 bold ideas** you'd actually pitch — plus one favorite fleshed out into a mini pitch. No building. No connectors. Pure creative fuel. 💡⚡

| 🏆 Level | ⏱️ Time | 🧰 Builder | 📦 Data you need |
|---|---|---|---|
| 🔵 Explore | ~10 min | **Idea Generation** (Google-made agent) | Nothing! Just your imagination 🧠 |

---

## 🎬 The story

TechBond makes seriously strong glue. 💪 But here's the problem: the brand feels *old*. The marketing team wants to win over **younger, eco-conscious customers** — people who care about sustainability, design, and doing things differently. They need fresh ideas, fast, and they're out of them.

Enter **Idea Generation** — a Google-made agent whose job is to turn a goal into candidate ideas, rank them, and help you pick a winner. You'll give it a creative challenge, refine the requirements, then flesh out the best idea. Let's make some magic. ✨

---

## 🛠️ Build it (follow along)

**Step 1 — Open the agent (zero setup 🎉).**
In your Gemini Enterprise app, open **Idea Generation** from the navigation / gallery. It's already built and ready. Nothing to create.

**Step 2 — Choose sources.**
Click **Sources** and choose any sources your tenant offers for ideation. For this lab, it's fine to use only your prompt if no workshop source is relevant.

**Step 3 — Set the goal.**
Give it a clear creative brief. Paste this:

> *"Give me 10 product or campaign ideas for TechBond to win younger, eco-conscious customers using our adhesive technology. Mix product ideas and marketing campaigns. Keep each one to one punchy line."*

Click **Submit**. Idea Generation drafts requirements for the session.

**Step 4 — Refine the requirements.**
Review the suggested requirements. Add one constraint to make the ideas more useful:

> *"Make at least 3 ideas bold and futuristic, but keep the target audience younger, eco-conscious customers."*

When the requirements look right, click **Start session**. The agent generates ideas and ranks them.

**Step 5 — Pick a favorite and flesh it out.**
Found one that makes you grin? Ask the agent to turn it into a mini pitch:

> *"Take idea #[your pick] and flesh it out into a mini pitch: the concept, who it's for, why it wins younger eco-conscious customers, and a catchy name."*

Now you've got a real, presentable concept — not just a one-liner. 🎤

**Step 6 — Make it pitch-ready.**
Polish it: *"Now write a 3-sentence version I could say out loud in a meeting."* Boom — an elevator pitch you could deliver tomorrow. 💼

## 💡 Stuck? Open a hint

<details>
<summary><strong>Hint 1 — I can't find the Idea Generation agent</strong></summary>

It's a Google-made agent, so look in the navigation / gallery of your Gemini Enterprise app — not under the agents *you* created. Search for **"Idea Generation."** If you can't see it, ask your facilitator whether it's enabled for the tenant.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Hint 2 — The ideas feel generic or boring</strong></summary>

Two fixes:
1. **Give it constraints.** Generic brief = generic ideas. Name the audience ("younger, eco-conscious"), the asset ("our adhesive technology"), and the format ("one punchy line each").
2. **Push back.** Just reply: *"Too safe — make them bolder and more specific to adhesives."* The more you steer, the better it gets. It's a partner, not a vending machine. 🤝
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Hint 3 — A great brainstorming recipe</strong></summary>

The magic formula: **how many + for whom + using what + in what format.**
> *"Give me [10] ideas for [audience] to [goal] using [TechBond's adhesive tech]. Format: one punchy line each. Make [3] of them wild."*

Then iterate: *"more like #4," "weirder," "now flesh out #7."* Brainstorming is a conversation, not a single shot. 🔁
</details>

<summary><strong>✅ Show me a full solution</strong></summary>

1. Open **Idea Generation**.
2. Pick sources if prompted.
