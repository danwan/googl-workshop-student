# 🔵 Lab 5.2 — The Idea Machine

> **Your mission:** Generate and rank ten ideas for younger, eco-conscious TechBond customers, then develop one winner into a pitch. 💡⚡

| 🏆 Level | ⏱️ Time | 🧰 Agent | 📦 Data you need |
|---|---|---|---|
| 🔵 Explore | ~10 min | **Idea Generation** (Google-made agent, Preview) | No files required |

> ⚠️ **Availability gate:** Idea Generation is Preview, available only in the Global region, and unavailable in Gemini Enterprise Frontline. If it is missing from **Agents → Made by Google**, use the fallback in Hint 1 and tell your facilitator.

---

## 🎬 The story

TechBond wants younger, eco-conscious customers but needs ideas that are specific enough to pitch. You provide the brief and judgment; the agent supplies options.

---

## 🎯 Your challenge

Use Idea Generation to:

- define a TechBond ideation goal in your own words;
- refine the proposed requirements before starting;
- generate and rank ten distinct product or campaign ideas;
- develop one winner into a named mini pitch and spoken elevator pitch.

Do not optimize for the first output. Add a meaningful constraint, challenge generic ideas, and choose the winner yourself.

## ✅ You did it when…

- [ ] You have ten distinct, ranked ideas mixing products and campaigns.
- [ ] The requirements include younger, eco-conscious customers and at least one constraint you added.
- [ ] One selected idea has a name, audience, value, and three-sentence spoken pitch.
- [ ] If the Preview agent was unavailable, you used the documented fallback and noted that it does not reproduce the ranked session experience.

---

## 💡 Stuck? Open a hint

<details>
<summary><strong>Hint 1 — Idea Generation is missing</strong></summary>

Confirm **Agent Gallery** is enabled and look under **Agents → Made by Google**. If the tenant, edition, or region does not expose it, open the regular Gemini Enterprise assistant. Ask it for ten ideas using the same brief, then ask it to score them against your requirements. This fallback produces useful ideas, but not Idea Generation's session and tournament behavior.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Hint 2 — Improve the brief</strong></summary>

Specify how many ideas, the audience, TechBond's adhesive technology, a mix of products and campaigns, and the desired format.
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Hint 3 — Push beyond safe ideas</strong></summary>

Require several bold or futuristic options, then respond to generic results with a concrete critique such as “more specific to adhesives.”
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>✅ Show me a full solution</strong></summary>

1. Open **Agents → Made by Google → Idea Generation**.
2. Submit: *“Generate ten product or campaign ideas for TechBond to win younger, eco-conscious customers using adhesive technology. Mix products and campaigns; keep each idea to one punchy line.”*
3. Add a requirement that at least three ideas are bold and futuristic, then start the session.
4. Review the ranking and select one idea.
5. Ask for its name, audience, concept, reason it wins, and a three-sentence spoken pitch.
6. If the agent is unavailable, perform the same sequence in the regular assistant and ask it to score every idea against the requirements.
</details>

---

## ✨ Creative finish (optional)

<details>
<summary><strong>Turn the winning idea into a Canvas pitch and concept image</strong></summary>

1. Open a regular Gemini Enterprise chat and choose **Canvas (Preview)**.
2. Ask for a three-slide pitch: problem, concept, and why it wins. Edit the result side by side with the chat.
3. Use **Image generation** to create one concept visual for the winner. Use the tenant's enabled default image model; do not depend on a Global-only model.
4. Check that the visual matches the pitch rather than adding new product claims.
</details>

## 🧠 What you just learned

Good ideation combines a clear brief, explicit constraints, model-generated options, and human judgment. Canvas and image generation can package the winner without changing the underlying evidence.
